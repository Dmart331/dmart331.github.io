About

Ihave always had a passion for complex problem solving. Early in my high school carreer I found my love for design. Thankfully, my school offered multiple graphic design classes and a few classes in web design. This pushed me to be a graphic design major at Middle Tennessee State University. I realized though, that doing design full time wasn't as fulfilling as I thought it would be. I desided that I would work on designs as a freelance artist while working as a mechanic during the day. It was here I really started to use my problem solving skills. After a few years at the repair shop, I decided to make a carreer move into banking. I stayed at the bank for a year and a half, when they were then bought by a larger bank and released a good number of employees... Time to make another move. Enter: Nashville Software School. This was everything I had been looking for. I was able now to use my problem solving skills and my graphic design skills to make websites and apps that have a chomprensive feel. I am extremely happy with the choices that have led me to where I am now and I wouldn't change one move that I have made.

I BELIEVE IN GREAT IDEAS

An important role of a developer is to be able to communicate with the client. I love being able to talk to a client about their idea and try to make that come to life.

Sometimes a client doesn't know what is possible or impossible, part of my job is finding a way to acheive the impossible. Everything is impossible until it gets accomplished. It all starts with an idea.
